About Geometry Dash

Geometry Dash is a rhythm-based platformer game that brings a full-blown test of timing, focus, and muscle memory to players. Loved by millions around the world, Geometry Dash is simple to learn, impossible to master, and endlessly addictive.

What Is Geometry Dash?

Geometry Dash is a high-speed, one-touch rhythm platformer where you control a cube (or other unlockable icons) through levels filled with deadly traps, spinning saws, spike pits, and gravity-shifting portals — all perfectly synced to explosive electronic music tracks.

There are no in-between moves, no second chances mid-run — just pure reaction, rhythm, and relentless retry until you nail it.

Developer: RobTop Games (founded by Swedish developer Robert Topala)

Initial Release Date: August 13, 2013 (iOS), followed by Android and PC/Steam versions.

Try another great version: Geometry Dash Lite.

How It Works

Tap to jump or fly — depending on your form.

Avoid obstacles — miss a single jump, and it’s game over.

Ride the rhythm — the music isn’t just background; it’s your guide.

Unlock new icons and colors — customize your cube as you progress.

Practice Mode — place checkpoints to learn tough parts before making the full run

Pro Tips to win

Always play with sound — The beat helps time your jumps.

Use Practice Mode — Place checkpoints to master difficult segments.

Watch replays or walkthroughs — See how top players handle tough sections.

Stay persistent — You will fail. You’ll even rage. But beating a hard level? Pure euphoria.

Try user levels — Some are fun and easy, others are “demon” difficulty — perfect for pros

The 21 Official Levels

Geometry Dash ships with 21 handcrafted levels that escalate from Easy all the way to Insane Demon. The first seven — Stereo Madness through Clutterfunk — teach core mechanics, while levels 11 through 16 introduce wave, robot, and UFO forms. The final act culminates in three Demon-rated levels: Clubstep, Theory of Everything 2, and the notorious Geometry Dash Deadlocked — each demanding near-perfect execution from start to finish.

Related Games Worth Playing

If you enjoy the core rhythm-platformer formula here, try the fire-themed spin-off Geometry Dash Meltdown for three brand-new levels, or challenge yourself with the pure wave mechanics in Geometry Dash Arrow. For a gentler experience that still captures the original fast-paced feel, Geometry Dash Lite offers the first seven official levels completely free in your browser.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many levels does Geometry Dash have?

The full Geometry Dash includes 21 official levels ranging from Easy difficulty all the way to Insane Demon. This browser version gives you access to all of them.

What is the difference between Geometry Dash and Geometry Dash Lite?

Geometry Dash Lite is the free version limited to 7 levels. The full Geometry Dash featured here includes all 21 official levels with no restrictions.

What is the hardest official level in Geometry Dash?

“Deadlocked” (level 21) is the hardest official stage, rated Insane Demon. You can also play it directly at geometrydash-lite2.poki2.online/geometry-dash-deadlocked.

Does this browser version support Practice Mode?

Yes. On the level select screen, choose Practice to place green checkpoints as you play, letting you retry difficult sections without restarting the full level.
